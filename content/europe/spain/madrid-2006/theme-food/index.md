A thematic look at everything we ate — from churros con chocolate at San Ginés at midnight to a Sunday lunch that lasted four hours.

## Churros con Chocolate

San Ginés is a Madrid institution open since 1894. We went twice: once on the first night, once at 11pm on the last. The chocolate is thick enough to stand a spoon in.

{{< photo src="churros.jpg" size="wide" caption="Churros at San Ginés. The chocolate is serious." >}}

## The Mercado de San Miguel

Newly renovated in 2009 — in 2006 it was still a working market, quieter, less tourist-facing. We bought olives, manchego, and a slab of jamón ibérico that we ate standing over the hotel room desk with a plastic knife.

## Sunday Lunch

On Sunday we sat down at a restaurant on Calle Cava Baja at 2pm and did not leave until after 6. This is not unusual in Spain. The cocido madrileño came in three courses, the way it is supposed to: broth first, then chickpeas and vegetables, then the meat.
